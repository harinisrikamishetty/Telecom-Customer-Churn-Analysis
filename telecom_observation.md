# TELECOM CUSTOMER CHURN ANALYSIS – SIMPLE INSIGHTS

## 1. Overall Customer Analysis

The company has a total of 7,032 customers. Out of them, 1,869 customers left the service. The churn rate is 26.5%, which means around 1 out of every 4 customers stopped using the service.

---

## 2. Contract Type Analysis

Customers with Month-to-Month contracts left the service more compared to customers with long-term contracts.

* Month-to-Month – 1,655 customers left
* One-Year Contract – 166 customers left
* Two-Year Contract – 48 customers left

This shows that customers with long-term contracts are more likely to stay with the company.

---

## 3. Payment Method Analysis

Customers using Electronic Check payment methods left the company more than other payment method users.

* Electronic Check – highest churn
* Mailed Check – lower churn
* Bank Transfer and Credit Card – lower churn

This means automatic payment methods help in keeping customers longer.

---

## 4. Internet Service Analysis

Most customers are using Fiber Optic internet service.

* Fiber Optic – 3,096 customers
* DSL – 2,416 customers
* No Internet Service – 1,520 customers

Fiber Optic users also had the highest number of churn customers.

---

## 5. Gender Analysis

Male and female customers left the company almost equally.

* Male churn customers – 930
* Female churn customers – 939

So, gender does not affect churn much.

---

## 6. Senior Citizen Analysis

Non-senior citizens left the service more than senior citizens.

* Non-Senior Citizens – 1,393 churn customers
* Senior Citizens – 476 churn customers

This means younger customers are more likely to leave the service.

---

## 7. Monthly Charges Analysis

Customers who left the company paid higher monthly charges.

* Customers who stayed – average charge 61.3
* Customers who left – average charge 74.4

Higher monthly charges may be one of the reasons for customer churn.

---

## 8. Contract and Monthly Charges

Month-to-Month customers pay slightly higher monthly charges compared to long-term contract customers.

* Month-to-Month – 66.3
* One-Year – 65.07
* Two-Year – 60.8

Long-term contract customers generally pay lower charges.

---

## 9. Customer Tenure Analysis

Customers who stayed with the company had longer tenure.

* Customers who stayed – 37.6 months
* Customers who left – 17.9 months

New customers are more likely to leave early.

---

## 10. Paperless Billing Analysis

Customers using paperless billing left more compared to paper billing customers.

* Paperless Billing churn customers – 1,400
* Paper Billing churn customers – 469

This shows that paperless billing customers had higher churn.

---

## 11. Tech Support Analysis

Customers without tech support left the service more.

* Without Tech Support – 1,446 churn customers
* With Tech Support – 310 churn customers

Providing better tech support may help reduce churn.

---

## 12. Online Security Analysis

Customers without online security services had higher churn.

* Without Online Security – 1,461 churn customers
* With Online Security – 295 churn customers

Online security services help in retaining customers.

---

## 13. Streaming Services Analysis

Streaming services did not show a major impact on churn.

* Without Streaming – 942 churn customers
* With Streaming – 814 churn customers

Both values are somewhat similar, so streaming alone does not affect churn much.

---

# FINAL CONCLUSION

The analysis shows that customers with:

* Month-to-Month contracts
* Higher monthly charges
* Electronic Check payments
* Fiber Optic internet services
* No Tech Support
* No Online Security

are more likely to leave the company.

The company can reduce churn by improving customer support, encouraging long-term contracts, improving Fiber Optic service quality, and providing better security services.
